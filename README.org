#+title: README

* Episteme

#+begin_quote
Epistēmē (ἐπιστήμη) is the Greek word for knowledge or understanding: the
theoretical, reasoned grasp of how and why things are. Aristotle contrasts it
with /praxis/ (action) and /technē/ (craft).
#+end_quote

Following the spirit of this word, this collection of org-roam notes on
different subjects forms my personal map of knowledge --- the theoretical half
of a pair whose practical counterpart lives in the =praxis= repo (possessions,
houses, trips, the logistics of daily life).

The specific selection of topics and the reason behind them may vary; usually I
write about what I want to understand deeper, either the topic itself or its
connections to things in another subject.

* Structure

org-roam notes, each its own file with an =:ID:= + =#+title=, linked by id-links
(the graph, not the folders, is what matters). Folders are kept to one shallow
level of broad domain --- =math/=, =physics/=, =philosophy/=, =ml/=,
=linguistics/=, =languages/=, =telecom/=, =GIICC/=, =Libros/=, =Museos/=,
=meditation/=, =audiovisual/=. Cross-cutting themes are better expressed with
org-roam tags than with deeper nesting.

* Support files

- =setup.org= --- shared LaTeX preamble (theorem environments, packages),
  pulled into math/physics notes via =#+SETUPFILE: ../setup.org=.
- =citations.bib= --- bibliography for =[cite:@key]= references. This is the
  *curated* slice (works I actually cite); the full Zotero library mirror lives
  in the separate =bibliotheca= repo.
- =.literature-notes/= --- one note per reference. Also holds
  =templates/zotero-emacs-synergy.org=, the plan for the live Zotero <-> Emacs
  citation pipeline (Better BibTeX + Citar + citar-org-roam), still to implement.
- =.attachments/= --- images embedded in notes (linked with =[[file:...]]=).

* Emacs

- =org-roam-directory= -> this repo (=~/knowledge/episteme=).
- =org-cite-global-bibliography= -> =("~/knowledge/episteme/citations.bib")=.
